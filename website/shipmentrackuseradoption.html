Problem Statement

The introduction of the new shipment tracking platform saw lower-than-expected adoption as users clung to outdated but familiar tools. The challenge lay not only in modernizing the interface but also in understanding users' attachment to the old tools and designing an equally intuitive experience that offered improved efficiency and clarity.

User Segments

Logistics (Primary )

Logistics coordinators are responsible for monitoring shipment progress and managing exceptions. They rely on real-time visibility to ensure goods reach their destination as scheduled.

Operation Managers (Secondary)

Operations managers oversee performance at a higher level, tracking multiple shipments and monitoring key metrics. Their main frustration stemmed from fragmented data and inconsistent user experiences across tools.

Customer Support (Internal)

Customer support agents use the same systems to assist clients in tracing or verifying shipments. They need fast lookup capabilities and clear visibility into shipment milestones to provide accurate responses.

Google Analytics Insights

Behavioral data analysis revealed key usability gaps that were limiting adoption and trust in the platform.

1. Feature Discoverability

Click tagging in strategic areas—such as Shipment Details and Map View—showed that many users struggled to find, or were unaware of, critical features that could enhance shipment monitoring and decision-making.

2. Display and Accessibility Issues

Usage data showed that while most users operated on 1920×1080 screens, approximately 40% used smaller resolutions (1440×1024 or 1280×832). Because the interface was designed for a fixed resolution, those users experienced cropped layouts, overlapping elements, and constrained navigation—revealing a clear gap in accessibility and overall usability.

Design Opportunity

Improve adaptive layout design and enhance feature discoverability to increase adoption, trust, and seamless usability across different user environments.

Adoption Strategy Summary — improving navigation, visibility, and accessibility.

To increase adoption of the shipment-tracking tool, we focused on strengthening discoverability, clarity, and usability. Entry points across the platform were restructured to align with user goals, making navigation more intuitive and consistent. We introduced a well-defined URL structure and intuitive navigation system so users could easily share and revisit specific pages, improving collaboration and trust. At the same time, the interface was rebuilt with responsive layouts, ensuring smooth performance and visual clarity across different resolutions. Together, these improvements directly addressed the main pain points—limited customization, poor visibility of items, and fragmented access resulting in a platform that felt cohesive, efficient, and easier to adopt.

Increasing Discoverability

Turning Complex Data into Clear Structure

By simply improving how the product was exposed, through clearer hierarchy, strategic placement of entry points, and subtle cues to highlight new features; we achieved a 20% increase in adoption. This showed how small, deliberate changes in structure and visibility can have a major impact on engagement, it makes users curious, confident, and more likely to explore.

Making more efficient use of space

Customization is King

We introduced customizable layouts to accommodate different user workflows. This flexibility let users emphasize what mattered most—reporting results, auditing shipment details, or monitoring operations across North America through a map view.

Data Tailored to Its Usage

Turning Complex Data into Clear Structure

While users wanted access to more data at once, the previous presentation lacked a clear navigational structure, which made it difficult to explore information intuitively. To address this, we reorganized the shipment details into four main sections instead of six, removing duplicate data and grouping related information logically. Each section was renamed using industry-specific shipment terminology, helping users instantly recognize familiar concepts and move through the interface more naturally. This improved both readability and discoverability, allowing users to find what they needed faster without feeling overwhelmed.

Saved Searches, Tracking Tiles and Reports Were Merged as "Saved Queries"

The reporting workflow relied on three overlapping objects saved searches, tiles, and reports—where saved searches effectively served as reports, and tiles were merely their graphical representations we combine them all in one single module, where were used as the trigger to run, share, save, overwrite, modify, etc... In addition to that, all the panels turned into collapsable areas that were taking place in different ways taking the right real state, giving priority to the main information and keeping secondary items in reduced areas.

Temperature Monitoring Section

By managing temperature-monitored shipments in a dedicated section, we provided users with a clearer overview to quickly identify anomalies and investigate issues in detail when needed. The temperature view included a customizable date-range selector, enabling users to analyze data with the desired precision and uncover trends or inconsistencies over time.

Simpler Experiences, Stronger Engagement

Within four weeks of release, a simplified information hierarchy and clearer navigation lowered cognitive load and encouraged repeat usage. The trend lines show sustained growth in new users alongside a steady reduction in bounce rate over four weeks. The layout uses neutral tones for body text with restrained accents for emphasis to maintain focus on the data.
